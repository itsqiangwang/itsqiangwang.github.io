For my typography class at UCLA's Design | Media Arts department during Spring 2017, I was assigned the task of choosing a song to create series of graphic assets for and a typeface to use for all the projects throughout the course.

I chose the song Chelsea Hotel No. 2 by Leonard Cohen and the typeface Clarendon. This quarter-long project resulted in the following products: a compiled publication with 40 single cover designs + an alphabet + a poster + a double album cover.

All products listed above are presented with mock-ups.

THE BOOK // Type of Music: 40 Single Covers for Chelsea Hotel No. 2
The 40 single cover designs were divided into 8 separate assignments, with each assignment following a distinct set of parameters.
This particular component of this project explored a methodology of the design process where I was given a very restricted and simple set of rules to work with at the very beginning to more free and complex ones towards the end. Both formal and expressive typography were explored throughout this process. The result is a publication compiling all 40 designs created using Adobe Indesign.
Book format: 8.5" x 11", hardcover
Single cover format: 7" x 7"

THE ALPHABET
I wanted to implement elements of a hotel into my customized alphabet. I took the idea of hotels being structures that are basically extrusions of some shape and proceeded to extrude the typeface to give it a three-dimensional, industrial and building-like appearance.

THE POSTER
poster format: 24" x 36"

THE ALBUM
For the album cover, each student was asked to use pictures of themselves that depict their relationships with their songs, whether it would be what the student is doing / feeling when listening to the song or where the student is at while listening to the song. The imageries of my choosing depict the 'action' aspect -- I find myself sometimes daydreaming and other times falling asleep when listening to Chelsea Hotel No. 2.

Course taught by Willem Henri Lucas + Amanda Stojanov at UCLA Design | Media Arts
